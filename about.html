About Indian Food

What is so unique about the Indian food? Well, it is the spices.

Spices have been used in Indian cooking for thousands of years. Spices provide flavor and color and act as preserving agents.

Indian spices are used in different forms such as roots, fruits, barks, flowers, dried seeds, leaves, whole, grounded, etc.

Some dishes are flavored with the seeds and some are with the paste of handful spices.

Common spices used

Common seeds used in Indian cooking are coriander, poppy seeds, sesame seeds, dill, fennel, fenugreek, mustard, etc.

Some leaves such as coriander, mint, Basil, curry leaves, bay leaf, peppermint leaves etc. are used for bringing distinctive flavor in Indian food.

Fruits and flowers such as vanilla, rose, juniper, mace, nutmeg, Rose etc. are also used as spices.

Ginger, Garlic, cloves, mango powder, onion powder, turmeric, black pepper etc. are also used to enhance the taste and flavors of Indian food.
